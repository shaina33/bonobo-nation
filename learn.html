Many writers and documentary filmmakers have contributed to my education for this project – indeed, far too many to list here. So, instead I have curated a selection of what seem to me the best & most interesting items from both academic and popular sources to jump start the process of compiling one's own “Top Ten”.

  1. This 1997 article started it all: Bonobo Sex and Society.
  2. An international panel, including Frans de Waal, presents on PBS.
  3. New York Times - In the Bonobo World, Female Camaraderie Prevails
  4. The New Yorker - Swingers: Bonobos are celebrated as peace-loving, matriarchal, and sexually liberated. Are they?
  5. TED Talks - Frans de Waal: Moral behavior in animals
  6. TED Talks - Susan Savage Rumbaugh: The gentle genius of bonobos
  7. Sex and strife: post-conflict sexual contacts in bonobos 2015, Behaviour, volume 152, pp 313-334. Zanna Clay, Frans B. M. de Waal (Yerkes National Primate Research Center)
  8. Obstacles and catalysts to peaceful coexistence in chimpanzees and bonobos 2016, Behaviour, volume 153, pp 1293-1330. Zanna Clay (University of Birmingham), Takeshi Furuichi (Kyoto University), Frans B. M. de Waal (Yerkes National Primate Research Center)
  9. Bonobos Respond to Distress in Others: Consolation across the Age Spectrum 2013, PLOS ONE, volume 8, issue 1. Zanna Clay, Frans B. M. de Waal (Yerkes National Primate Research Center)
  10. What do Bonobos Sound Like? (hear real audio clips!)
